DESCRIPTION


To study climate change, scientists often look to the Arctic. Extracting long core samples from deep beneath the ice sheet reveals chemical constitutions of years past. This field station serves as a seasonal lab for researchers gathering core samples for Columbiaâ€™s Lahmont-Doherty repository.


The lab is uniquely suited for the harsh climate of northern Greenland. With a heavy poured-concrete base, the lightweight pre-fabricated aluminum frame sits elevated from the ground and accumulating snow. The structure doubles as a vertical-axis wind turbine, generating sustainable energy to power the station while researchers work inside.
